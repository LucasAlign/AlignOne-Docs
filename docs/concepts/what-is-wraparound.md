# What is WrapAround?

AlignOne helps a foster or adoptive **family** and the people around them coordinate care in
one place. That circle of support — the family plus everyone helping them — is a
**WrapAround care circle**.

## The people in a care circle

- **The family** — the foster/adoptive parents and children being served. In AlignOne the
  family *is* the care circle; everything else is organized around it.
- **The advocate** — the case lead who coordinates support for a family on behalf of a
  church. One advocate looks after the families their church serves.
- **Volunteers** — people who sign up to help a specific family with real, practical tasks.
- **Program staff** — the non-profit team who set families up and keep an eye across
  everyone.

## How care gets coordinated

- **Needs** — a family's specific requests for help, like a meal, a ride, or supplies.
  Volunteers **claim** the ones they can take. → [Claim a need](../how-to/needs/claim-a-need.md)
- **Schedules** — planned events and recurring commitments on the family's calendar.
- **Messages** — private conversations among the people in a care circle.
- **Posts** — updates shared with the circle.
- **Training** — modules that prepare volunteers and advocates to serve well.

## A care circle is private

What you can see depends on your role — a volunteer sees only their own family, while
program staff see everyone. See [Roles & who sees what](roles-and-visibility.md) for the
details.
